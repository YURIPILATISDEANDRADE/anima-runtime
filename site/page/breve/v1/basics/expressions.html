Expressions

Expressions are the building blocks of your code. These can be the following:

Unary Expressions: Comprised of inversion (-<expr>), positration (+<expr>), pre-increment (++<expr>), pre-decrement (--<expr>), post-increment (<expr>++), post-decrement (<expr>--), logical negation (!<expr>), bitwise negation (~<expr>), sizing (sizeof <expr>).

Binary Expressions: Wrapped within parens, and comprised of arithmetic operations (e.g. <expr> + <expr>), logic operations (e.g. <expr> && <expr>), bitwise operations (e.g. <expr> ^ <expr>), casting (e.g. <expr> as <type>), null coalescence (e.g. <expr> or <expr>), structured subscription (e.g. <expr> : <expr>), assignment chaining (e.g. <expr> = <expr>), comparisons (e.g. <expr> > <expr>).

Ternary Expressions: Also wrapped within parens, and comprised of inline valuing (e.g. <expr> if <expr> else <expr>).

Variadic Expressions: Also wrapped within parens, and comprised of the comma operator (e.g. (<expr> , <expr> [, ...])).

Function calls: Like ones seen previously. Arguments are passed within parens.

Block declarations: Wrapped within curly brackets, and contains one or more expressions.

As for each class of operation:
